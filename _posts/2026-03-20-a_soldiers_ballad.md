<div style="text-align: center;">

I wore the call like borrowed skin,<br>
"Serve thy nation, cleanse the sin."<br>
But duty whispered in disguise---<br>
And led me blind with open eyes.<br><br>

I shot a boy whose hands were inked,<br>
His gaze met mine---our fates linked.<br>
His mother's scream became my thread,<br>
A lullaby I sing in dread.<br><br>

I silenced poets, burned their page,<br>
Mistook their words for rebel rage.<br>
No gun they raised, no war they waged---<br>
Just truths too loud to keep uncaged.<br><br>

They pinned a medal on my chest,<br>
A shining badge that won't let rest.<br>
Each star a mark I can't erase---<br>
An honor earned in dark disgrace.<br><br>

They spoke of pride and sacrifice,<br>
But never told me peace has a price.<br>
Now dreams return in uniform,<br>
And every night becomes a storm.<br><br>

This ballad plays in broken loops,<br>
Of war not won but buried truths.<br>
I bore a flag that bore a lie---<br>
And now I'm left too dry to cry.<br>

</div>
