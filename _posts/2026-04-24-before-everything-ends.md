<div style="text-align: center;">

Dad, I wasn't your rising sun,<br>
Not the race you cheered, the game I won.<br>
Your silence carved a deeper scar<br>
Than any word or shouting war.<br><br>

At night, I heard Mom's muffled cries,<br>
My sister's hush, the lull of lies.<br>
Their grief wore veils of normal days---<br>
A house of whispers, pain ablaze.<br><br>

I bore your dreams like leaden weights,<br>
Chasing smiles at locked gates.<br>
Each nod withheld, each glance a toll---<br>
I paid in silence, never whole.<br><br>

I learned to laugh through phantom pain,<br>
To stitch my wounds with scripted strain.<br>
Now silence presses, cold and sheer---<br>
Can you taste my final tear?<br><br>

Would you recall the truths I faked?<br>
The nights my quiet spirit ached?<br>
I craved a pause, not pity's eyes---<br>
Just space to fall without disguise.<br><br>

Don't mock this hush I leave behind---<br>
It's not surrender, but a kind<br>
Of peace, I never dared to claim---<br>
Not Romeo, but just a name.

</div>
