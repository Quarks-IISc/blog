Poets: Sankalp Malkapure and Kushal Chaudhari

<div style="text-align: center;">

(with broken eyes i awoke)<br><br>

the dawn was a jagged line of blue,<br>
with an eager heart i went to prepare,<br>
to dress in a skin of scholar<br>
new and untouched.<br><br>

as i stepped in, a gust of wind hit my face,<br>
a rush of scent, of movement.<br><br>

(the air hangs heavy, thick and solemn,<br>
these new, unproven choices - i condemn).<br><br>

the freshness of trees - the green breath of a legacy,<br>
and the wide canopy easing my heart.<br><br>

(the scent of cement made my nerves tight,<br>
i felt a fierce urge to get out of this plight).<br><br>

rubbing my legs in the sand,<br>
i stepped into the line, smiling harder, quietly admiring all of it.<br><br>

(the pressure tightened my chest, i pushed the faces out of sight,<br>
keeping me, from myself, out of a fight).<br><br>

i went in, (i accepted).<br>
i enjoyed, (i feared).<br><br>

i walked the aisle - quiet discipline of space<br>
the order hums through the glazed wood,<br>
speaks in polished floors and shouts through the light.<br><br>

(i shrank in my own skin<br>
stood aside in the borrowed light -<br>
a mere shadow<br>
beside the brighter sight).<br><br>

a standout caught me in the room;<br>
among the triumphs, introduced me as absence.<br>
restlessness held my wrists<br>
yet i let it stay.<br><br>

(loving the silence, accepting the misery,<br>
i turned my heart towards the glory).<br><br>

going back to her - in small font.<br>
sharing her happiness,<br>
climbing out of the depths,<br>
i rejected the daunting gaze.<br><br>

(accepting the scars.<br>
joy lies in the misery -<br>
behold, an elegant fallacy).<br>

</div>
