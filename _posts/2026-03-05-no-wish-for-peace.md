Poet: Manas Kaushik Das

<div style="text-align: center;">

I tell myself that peace is not for me,<br>
For guilt, a tempest, gnaws my being's roots,<br>
Old sins, like shards, carve deep within my soul,<br>
And anguish drowns each breath I briefly draw.<br><br>

For guilt, a tempest, gnaws my being's roots,<br>
Yet I press on, to tear through my dark void,<br>
And anguish drowns each breath I briefly draw,<br>
Despair proclaims redemption but a dream.<br><br>

Yet I press on, to tear through my dark void,<br>
These chains of old, I cast them to the wind,<br>
Despair proclaims redemption but a dream,<br>
And questions if my strife holds any worth.<br><br>

These chains of old, I cast them to the wind,<br>
Yet I defy the flame, scorn fate's decree,<br>
And question if my strife holds any worth,<br>
But through this storm, a star begins to gleam.<br>

</div>
