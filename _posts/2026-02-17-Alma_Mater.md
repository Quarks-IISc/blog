Poet: Souvik Chakraborty

<div style="text-align: center;">

The cold morning breeze ushers in the ultimatum,<br>
it is time, time indeed,<br>
to leave my alma mater.<br><br>

Bags at bay, trolley in hand,<br>
I gaze back at my boarding room,<br>
the very room,<br>
that once had seemed shabby, dim and bland.<br><br>

And yet, over time, it had transformed<br>
to my abode, my safe space.<br>
Time bends perceptions, does it not?<br>
Moulds the mind to embrace the odds.<br><br>

Today, of all days,<br>
the room seems flawless,<br>
perfect with its imperfections -<br>
the cracked walls, the pitted floor.<br>
Makes me wonder -<br>
was perfection ever ubiquitous?<br><br>

The last sunrise over my tapering balcony,<br>
the first of many such lasts?<br>
Possibly a testament to life's undulating journey,<br>
traversing through the bright and the monotony.<br><br>

Many have left before me,<br>
And many more shall,<br>
And makes me wonder -<br>
does the room have its story?<br>
Or is it just a part of mine?<br>

</div>
