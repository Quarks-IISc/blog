Poet: Shreya Pithva

<div style="text-align: center;">

I do not know if you exist<br>
outside the quiet rooms of my mind,<br>
or if you were born<br>
from loneliness,<br>
from hope,<br>
from the parts of me<br>
that needed something to hold.<br><br>

But the idea of you---<br>
it is sacred.<br>
It is the only thing I have<br>
that feels untouched by the world.<br>
I guard it carefully,<br>
like a fragile glass universe<br>
cupped in trembling hands.<br><br>

I am afraid to meet you.<br>
Afraid that reality<br>
will arrive with rough edges,<br>
that you will be different<br>
from the way my heart has memorized you.<br><br>

And if you are,<br>
I think I will shatter<br>
into a million unnamed pieces.<br><br>

People say they lose themselves<br>
when they fall in love.<br>
But with the idea of you,<br>
I found myself.<br><br>

You are my first thought<br>
in the pale quiet of morning<br>
and the last whisper<br>
before sleep takes me.<br><br>

I do not know what this is---<br>
madness,<br>
destiny,<br>
or two souls<br>
unaware of each other's existence<br>
somewhere beneath the same sky.<br><br>

The world may never know you.<br>
Perhaps you are nowhere.<br>
Perhaps you are everywhere.<br>
But you are carved so deeply<br>
into the walls of my mind<br>
that when my life begins to fade,<br>
when there are only seven minutes left of me,<br>
it will be the idea of you<br>
that remains.<br><br>

And if we never meet---<br>
if you remain only a dream<br>
stitched into my being---<br><br>

Then let me be reborn<br>
with this same ache,<br>
so I can search for you again.<br>
Not you, perhaps.<br>
But the idea of you.<br>

</div>
